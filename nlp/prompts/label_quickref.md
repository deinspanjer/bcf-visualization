# BCF Label Quick Reference

## Layer A — Roll events (prose only; span smallest clause carrying the event)

ROLL_HIT — Forge reaches into a constellation and successfully grabs a mote (perk acquired); include adjacent perk name if in the same clause — ex: "the Forge snatched Perfect Pitch from the wheel" | NOT the end-of-chapter "Jumpchain abilities" catalog block
ROLL_MISS — Forge reaches but fails to grab; perk cost exceeded banked CP — ex: "the Forge strained toward the node but couldn't quite grasp it" | NOT a passing mention that a perk exists or is desired

## Layer B — Narrative anchors (prose only; span the clause or phrase carrying the signal)

PERK_REFERENCE — Named mention of a specific PERK in narrative body prose, marking that the perk is in Joe's possession/context — covers retrospective references, in-action mentions, and post-hoc naming — ex: "the new power was called Mixing Mixtures", "Iron Lung had been useful", "I activated my Workshop" | NOT a constellation name; NOT the end-of-chapter catalog block; span tightly on the named substring (no surrounding "the"/"power")
CONSTELLATION_REFERENCE — Named mention of a specific CONSTELLATION in narrative body prose; usually co-occurs with ROLL_HIT/ROLL_MISS clauses — ex: "the Size constellation passed by", "Time constellation missed a connection", "from my Personal Reality constellation" | NOT a perk name; span tightly on the constellation name (e.g. "Size", "Time", "Personal Reality") without surrounding "the"/"constellation"
PRESENCE_ACTION — Non-Joe-POV sections only: Joe (or Mr. Duris / Apeiron) as subject of an in-scene action verb in the narrative present — ex: "'Yeah,' Apeiron said", "Joe walked into the room" | NOT POV-internal recall ("she wondered what Joe was doing"), off-scene past ("they'd talked to Apeiron yesterday"), or anything in a Joe-POV section
DATE_REF — In-world calendar reference in body prose — ex: "April 14th", "early January 2011" | NOT footers, edit dates, or sponsor blurbs
TIME_OF_DAY — In-world diurnal reference in body prose — ex: "after sunset", "around noon" | NOT "morning person" or non-temporal uses
DURATION — In-world passage of time — ex: "two hours later", "after a week", "the next day" | NOT distance metaphors
FLASHBACK_CUE — In-prose marker of a shift to recollection — ex: "he remembered", "two years earlier" | NOT chapter-break-only signals
DILATION_CUE — In-prose marker of power-induced time dilation — ex: "a heartbeat that lasted minutes", "subjective hours compressed into seconds" | NOT ordinary time expressions
AUTHOR_NOTE — Parenthesized aside from the author to the reader, embedded in the prose; downstream subtracted from CP-eligible word counts — span the WHOLE block including parens — ex: "(Author's note: chapter split this week — wrapping up next time)" | NOT in-character thoughts in parens; only out-of-story author-to-reader notes
